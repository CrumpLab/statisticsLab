Answering questions with data: Lab Manual

Chapter 11 distributions

Using distributions to model problems

  1. Coin flipping
  1. You flip a fair coin 10 times. What is the probability you will get 5 heads and 5 tails? Estimate by simulation
  2. You flip a fair coin 10 times. What is the probability you will get 3 heads and 7 tails? Estimate by simulation
  3. You flip a fair coin 10 times. What are the probabilities of every possible outcome of heads and tails. Estimate by simulation, create a histogram showing the probabilities of each outcome. Create a table showing the probabilities of each outcome.
  4. You flip a fair coin 10 times. What is the probability you get 5 or more heads. Estimate by simulation.
  1. Dice rolling
  1. You have a fair die with 6 sides, numbered 1 to 6. What is the probability of rolling any given number. Estimate by simulation, display your results in a histogram.
  2. For a fair sided die with 6 sides, numbered 1 to 6. What is the probability of rolling a 5 or higher. Estimate by simulation.
  3. You have two dice, each with 6 sides, numbered 1 to 6. You roll both dice and add the two outcomes together. What are the probabilites of all the possible outcomes. Estimate by simulation, show your work in a histogram. Which outcome is the most likely?
  1. Normal distribution
  1. All of the people living on a small island have a mean height of 60 inches. Heights are distributed normally, with a standard deviation of 10. If you take a sample of 20 people at random, on average, what is the mean height of the sample? Estimate by simulation.
  2. From above, if you take a sample of 100 people at random, on average, what is the mean height of the sample? Estimate by simulation.
  3. From above, if you take a sample of 20 people at random, on average, what is the standard deviation of the sample? Estimate by simulation.
  4. From above, if you take a sample of 100 people at random, on average, what is the standard deviation of the sample? Estimate by simulation.
  5. From above, if you take a sample of 20 people at random, on average, what is the shortest, and tallest height you would expect from your sample? Estimate by simulation.
  6. What happens to the average mean of your sample as you increase sample size from 10 to 100 in steps of 10? Demonstrate by simulation.
  7. What happens to the average standard deviation of your sample as you increase sample size from 10 to 100 in steps of 10? Demonstrate by simulation.
  8. If you start with a sample size of 10, and keep increasing in steps of 10, on average what sample size do you need so that your average standard deviation of your sample is less than 1. Estimate by simulation. Create a graph plotting the average standard deviation of your sample as sample-size increases. Use this graph to estimate the the sample size needed.
